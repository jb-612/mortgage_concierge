# Mortgage Guide

Purchasing, renovating, or building a new home is a significant life event.
In Israel, as in many countries worldwide, most homes are purchased or built using a housing loan – a "mortgage": a bank loan for housing that allows the purchase of an apartment or self-construction of a residential home, even if you do not yet have the full required amount.
Additionally, a mortgage can be obtained against the lien of a residential apartment for various non-business purposes.

## In this guide, you will find relevant information for obtaining a mortgage.

A housing loan (mortgage) according to the Bank of Israel's directives is a loan not intended for business purposes and is designated for financing one of the following objectives:

1.  Purchase or leasehold of a residential apartment, its construction, expansion, or renovation.
2.  Purchase of a plot for building a residential apartment or purchasing rights in a residential apartment in exchange for key money.
3.  A loan for any purpose - secured by an existing residential apartment.
4.  Loan refinancing - a loan intended for financing the early repayment (full or partial) of a loan as mentioned in sections 1 and 2.

## Terms You Should Know

### Appraisal (Shma'ut)

Assessment of the property's value as determined by a real estate appraiser. This document serves as the basis for credit approval for the financing percentage determined for the mortgage. The amount approved for the loan will be according to the property value determined by the appraiser or the purchased property value (in case of purchase), whichever is lower.

### Rights Confirmation (Ishur Zchuyot)

Confirmation issued by the property owner, or an entity authorized on their behalf, Israel Land Authority ("RMI"), a contracting company, or another managing entity, detailing the rights registered for a specific property, such as: property details, rights holders in the property, liens, attachments, etc., registered against it.
In some cases, the Israel Land Authority does not manage the records for the property itself, but rather the contracting company that built the project or whoever was authorized for this purpose. These are called by RMI - "managing entity" (Gorem Meshaken). In such a case, a confirmation called "Notification of Rights Registration in Property" must be obtained from the Israel Land Authority, where RMI indicates who the managing entity is, as well as a rights confirmation from that managing entity, containing details about the status of the rights in the property.

### Tabu Extract (Nesach Tabu)

A document that is a copy of the land registration maintained in the Land Registry Office (Tabu) and attests to the rights related to a specific plot or sub-plot. The purpose of the document is to show who holds the rights to the property for which the mortgage is requested and to check if there are existing liens, attachments, or other rights on the property.

### Insurance Related to the Mortgage (Bituchim Agav Mashkanta)

As collateral for receiving the mortgage, you must insure yourselves with life insurance and insure the property with structure insurance, with the bank being the beneficiary for receiving the insurance proceeds. See further details below in the Insurance section.

### Registration of a Cautionary Note for the Bank (Rishum Hearat Azhara)

A cautionary note registered in the Land Registry Office (Tabu) indicating the seller's commitment to the borrower's bank not to transfer the rights in the buyer's name without registering a mortgage for the bank.

### Commitment to Register Mortgage (Hitchayvut LeRishum Mashkanta)

If your rights in the property are based on a contract, a commitment to register a mortgage is also required, whereby upon registration of ownership/leasehold rights in the Land Registry Office in your name, a mortgage will be registered in favor of the bank. The commitment will be given by whoever handles the registration of rights: the landowner, the seller, the Israel Land Authority, as applicable.

### Property Lien/Pledge (Mishkun HaNeches)

If your right is ownership or registered leasehold in the Tabu, the property lien is created by registering a mortgage deed in the Land Registry Office. If the rights are contractual (with a seller, with the Israel Land Authority), the lien on the rights in the property as collateral for the loan is created by a pledge agreement and registered with the Registrar of Pledges.

## So How Much Money Do You (Really) Need?

### What is the exact amount you will need on the way to your own home?

You already know the price of the house - but there are other factors that must be taken into account before taking a mortgage. Here is the important information for your service.

### Calculate the final cost of the transaction.

It turns out that there is a gap between the price presented to you and the amount you will actually pay, which affects the amount of the mortgage you need. Therefore, it is highly recommended to include the following expenses (all or part), depending on the specific transaction, in the equity amount:

*   Taxes related to the real estate transaction (purchase tax, betterment levy, etc.).
*   Payments to the Israel Land Authority (RMI).
*   Attorney's fees.
*   Moving costs.
*   Additional expenses such as: brokerage fees, renovation costs, etc. - as applicable to your specific transaction.

### How much money is available to you?

You've calculated the amount needed to buy a home? Excellent. Now you need to check how much money you have available today, how much you will need to borrow, and how much you will need to repay the bank each month.

### How much can you repay, each month, in the future?

You must match the mortgage amount to your repayment ability. Calculate your total joint monthly income (net), subtract monthly expenses, and ensure the mortgage repayment amount does not exceed your ability. When calculating the monthly amount available to you, it is recommended to consider future changes in income, for example: having children, career advancement on a known and fixed track, and other additional commitments.

#### Things it's important you know!

Additional expenses will be added to the price of the new apartment.
For example - attorney fees, purchase tax, various insurances, levies, and more. In short: when you determine the mortgage amount, consider the GROSS price of the apartment.
The profit will be your net.
Please note! For the purpose of determining the financing rate that the loan will constitute relative to the property's value, the bank examines the NET property price only, without the additional expenses mentioned above.

_The information provided is general information and explanation only._

## Loan Amount Limit Relative to Property Value (LTV)

Loan amount limit relative to property value: When submitting a mortgage application, the bank will examine, at its discretion, the loan amount it is willing to provide as a housing loan, provided that the ratio of the loan amount to the property value does not exceed the rates instructed by the Supervisor of Banks. Below is a table to help you understand the different scenarios and the maximum financing percentage (LTV) available in each case.

| Property Constitutes | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     | Maximum Financing Rate |
| :------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------- |
| **"Single Property"** | A single property as defined in the Land Taxation Law (Appreciation and Purchase), 5723-1963 (hereinafter: "Land Taxation Law") - a property purchased by an Israeli citizen and constitutes their only residential property in Israel as defined in the Land Taxation Law.                                                                                   | Up to 75% financing    |
| **"Replacement Property"** | A replacement property as defined in the Land Appreciation Tax Law - a residential property purchased by an Israeli citizen who owns another residential property which, were it not for the purchased property, would constitute a single property, and the borrower commits to sell it within the timeframes required by the Land Appreciation Tax Law. | Up to 70% financing    |
| **"Investment Property"** | An investment property – a property that is not a single property or a replacement property.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                | Up to 50% financing    |

*   Housing loan for purchasing a right in real estate: Single Property – 75% | Replacement Property – 70% | Investment Property – 50%

_The above information is subject to all laws and changes that may occur in the directives of the Supervisor of Banks. It is recommended to check with the bank close to the date the housing loan is required._

### A. Details regarding loan tracks available from bank funds not based on an eligibility certificate

#### Loans in New Shekels Linked to the CPI

In a loan linked to the Consumer Price Index (CPI), the monthly repayment amount will be updated monthly due to linkage to the CPI according to the rise or fall of the new index relative to the base index.
When dealing with CPI-linked loans, it is important to understand the following concepts regarding linkage terms:

*   **"Base Index"**: The index known at the time the loan is disbursed. However, in cases where the period between the loan disbursement date and the first repayment date is shorter or longer than one month, the base index will be determined by adjusting the known index, according to the directives of the Supervisor of Banks.
*   **"New Index"**: Means the index published most recently before the due date of any payment on account of principal and/or interest as stated in the loan agreement (if signed).

There are several CPI-linked loans offered by the bank:

##### Fixed Linked Loan - Fixed interest rate linked to the CPI - Shekeli:

*   Loan term from 4 to 30 years.
*   The interest rate in this track is fixed, and there is linkage to the CPI, so the monthly repayment will be updated monthly due to linkage to the CPI according to the rise or fall of the new index relative to the base index.
*   **Interest rate and its determination mechanisms:** The interest rate determined in the principal approval will apply if the loan track is fully disbursed during the validity period of the principal approval. If the loan is disbursed in parts, the interest rate for each of the additional parts of the loan will be determined according to a mechanism for determining interest rates in parts, as detailed in the principal approval and the loan agreement.

##### Variable Linked Loan, every 3, 5, or 10 years, based on government bonds - Variable interest rate every 3, 5, or 10 years based on CPI-linked Israeli government bonds:

*   Each of the tracks above - variable linked loan every 3 years or variable linked loan every 5 years or variable linked loan every 10 years based on CPI-linked Israeli government bonds, will be referred to hereinafter as "track" or "the track". If you choose more than one of these tracks, the following applies to each of the selected tracks accordingly.
*   **Loan Term:**
    *   Variable linked loan track every 3 years – Loan term from 6 to 30 years.
    *   Variable linked loan track every 5 years – Loan term from 10 to 30 years.
    *   Variable linked loan track every 10 years – Loan term from 20 to 30 years.
*   The interest rate in the selected track varies based on yields of CPI-linked Israeli government bonds, as detailed below.
*   In a CPI-linked loan, the monthly repayment amount will be updated monthly due to linkage to the CPI according to the rise or fall of the new index relative to the base index.
*   **Interest rate and its determination mechanisms:** The interest rate will change over the loan term according to the selected track (hereinafter: "**Interest Rate Change Date**"):
    *   Variable linked loan track every 3 years - the interest rate will change every 36 months.
    *   Variable linked loan track every 5 years - the interest rate will change every 60 months.
    *   Variable linked loan track every 10 years - the interest rate will change every 120 months.
*   The interest rate will change on the Interest Rate Change Date according to the mechanism described below.
*   In the selected track, the base interest rate is calculated based on CPI-linked bond yields, to which a fixed-rate interest margin will be added.
*   The base interest rate (to which a fixed-rate margin will be added) will be determined at the time of loan disbursement and at each interest rate change date as detailed above according to the selected track, based on the real yield in percentages derived from the zero-coupon yield curve estimate, of government bonds in the linked sector, traded on the Tel Aviv Stock Exchange, published two business days before the start of the interest period, by the Bank of Israel, in the "average type" calendar in the column referring to the "Term to Maturity (in years)" relevant according to the interest rate change period in the selected track (in the variable linked track every 3 years, find the information in the "Term to Maturity" column for 3 years; in the variable linked track every 5 years, the information will appear in the table in the column for 5 years; and in the variable linked track every 10 years, in the column for 10 years).

## Unique Products and Services Waiting for You at Poalim Mortgages

### Month Off from the Mortgage*

Want a break from the mortgage?
You can enjoy the "Month Off per Year" program.
Once per calendar year, you can choose one month where the mortgage repayment will be deferred. This solution is suitable, for example, for months with special events like going on vacation abroad, family events, expenses for summer camps, home renovations, etc.
During the deferral period, the monthly payment will not be made. The payment for that month will be deferred to the end of the loan term and added to the loan balance. The remaining loan term will be extended for each deferral. Suitable for borrowers taking loans from bank funds without a deferred payment component.

## Charges, Fees, and Expenses

Taking a mortgage involves paying fees and expenses.

### Expenses to be Paid for Creating Collateral

## Life Insurance and Structure Insurance Related to the Mortgage

As collateral for receiving the loan, you must insure yourselves with life insurance and insure the property with property structure insurance, with the bank being the irrevocable beneficiary for receiving the insurance proceeds.
Below is an explanation of the insurances you will be asked to provide:

#### Mortgage Life Insurance ("Risk"):

To secure the loan, you must insure yourselves with life insurance for the loan balance amount, for the entire loan period, designed to repay the loan balance in the unfortunate event of the borrower's death.
We recommend verifying with the insurer that the life insurance policy covers the full loan balance including default interest accrued on the loan and the balance created between the date of death and the date of loan repayment through the payment according to the policy. Otherwise, such insurance will result in partial insurance (under-insurance) and will not cover the full amounts remaining for repayment.

#### Structure Insurance:

You must insure the property pledged to the bank with structure insurance for the entire loan period, designed to repay the loan in the event of damage to the property. The policy must include coverage for earthquake and plumbing damage. A structure insurance policy must be provided to the bank each year upon expiration of the existing policy.
You can insure the property for the estimated loan balance amount, deducting the land value related to the property, plus up to 20% of the loan balance. Note that insurance as described above will result in partial insurance (under-insurance) and will reduce the compensation paid by the insurance company, if any, in the event of a covered damage incident.

*   **In case of purchasing a "first-hand" residential apartment (from a developer, contractor, etc.)** - you must provide the structure insurance policy no later than the date the seller committed to deliver the apartment to you, or the date the apartment was actually delivered to you, whichever is earlier. If the sales contract does not specify such a date and/or if a date was specified but you ask the bank to set a different date (for example, if delivery of the apartment is delayed), the bank will respond to your request if it does not increase the risk to the bank.
*   **When taking a loan for self-construction** - you must notify the bank of the estimated completion date of construction, at which time you will provide the bank with a structure insurance policy. If the borrower does not contact the bank regarding this matter, the date for providing the structure insurance policy will be set 12 months from the date of receiving the building permit.
*   **In any other case** - structure insurance must be provided to the bank at the time the loan is granted.

For this purpose, you will be required to provide the bank with valid life and structure insurance policies starting from the loan disbursement date (except in the case of purchasing a "first-hand" property and its lien or when taking a loan for self-construction where you will need to provide the structure insurance policy at the date specified above) and until its actual repayment, in which the bank will be irrevocably designated as the beneficiary to receive the insurance proceeds, and which include conditions necessary to secure the bank's rights.

The policies must include the insurer's commitment to notify the bank in writing 30 days in advance in case of policy cancellation.

#### Please note:

You can choose to arrange the life insurance and structure insurance through an external insurer or through the insurance agency, Poalim Mortgages Insurance Agency 2005 Ltd. (hereinafter: "Poalim Mortgages Insurance Agency"), which is an insurance agency fully owned by the bank, or combine the aforementioned insurers, all according to your choice.

#### Special Exemptions from the Insurance Obligation:

1.  The borrower is not required to have structure and life insurance if the total loans whose repayment is secured, among other things, by the property lien, does not exceed NIS 30,000, or their balance does not exceed NIS 30,000.
2.  The borrower is not required to have structure and life insurance if the entire loan is a grant given from state funds according to an eligibility certificate from the Ministry of Construction and Housing, but must insure their life to secure the balance of other loan tracks, if any, subject to the exemption mentioned in section 1 above).
3.  The borrower is not required to have structure insurance if and when the loan or its balance (principal, linkage differentials, interest, and incidentals as detailed in the agreement with the borrower) is lower than the land value alone (without the structure built on it).

#### Important to Know

The bank does not act as an insurance agent in the purchase of any insurance policy, including through Poalim Mortgages Insurance Agency, and the borrower's contract is solely with the insurer and the insurance agent chosen by the borrower. Furthermore, the bank is not responsible for the validity of the insurance policies, even if the bank is the beneficiary on the policies and even if the bank approved their receipt, and the responsibility for this rests entirely with the borrower.

## Repayment Arrangements and Setting the Repayment Date

Before receiving the loan, you must provide the bank with an authorization from the bank where your current account is managed, confirming that it has received an irrevocable instruction from you to honor the bank's debits presented monthly during the loan period.

### Setting the Contractual Repayment Date of the Loan:

**What is the monthly debit date on which you must pay the mortgage?**
The payment day each month on which the payment must be made (hereinafter: "Monthly Repayment Date") will be uniform for all components of the mortgage granted by the bank. You can choose the monthly debit date for loans in new shekels so that the Monthly Repayment Date will be on the 1st, 10th, or 15th of the month according to what is agreed between you and the bank.

**First Payment Date and Start of Loan Term:**
For housing loans in new shekels - unless otherwise specified in the loan agreement between you and the bank regarding payment deferral, the first Monthly Repayment Date will be determined as follows:

| Monthly Repayment Date | Actual Loan Disbursement Date | Loan Term Start Date          | Actual First Payment Date        |
| :--------------------- | :-------------------------- | :---------------------------- | :------------------------------- |
| 1st of the month       | 1st-15th of the month       | 1st of the disbursement month | 1st of the following month       |
|                        | 16th-31st of the month      | 1st of the following month    | 1st of the month after next    |
| 10th of the month      | 1st-15th of the month       | 10th of the disbursement month| 10th of the following month      |
|                        | 16th-31st of the month      | 10th of the following month   | 10th of the month after next   |
| 15th of the month      | 1st-15th of the month       | 15th of the disbursement month| 15th of the following month      |
|                        | 16th-31st of the month      | 15th of the following month   | 15th of the month after next   |

### Want to change the monthly debit date?

Once every four years between the months of April and August, you can approach the bank with a request to change the monthly debit date (e.g., to switch from a repayment date on the 1st of each month to the 10th of each month). The change is subject to the conditions set forth in the Banking Law (Customer Service), 5741-1981.

### Borrower's Right to Defer the Monthly Mortgage Payment Date

#### Deferral of Mortgage Payments

During the life of the loan, the borrower can defer the monthly mortgage payment dates, provided they request it and meet the following conditions:

1.  The borrower presents a document attesting that the circumstances described in one of the following sections have occurred:
    a. The borrower finished their employment in the year preceding the request and has not worked since (the 12 months prior to submitting the request for payment deferral);
    b. The borrower became ill or injured and consequently is unfit for work for a continuous period exceeding three months;
    c. The borrower gave birth, less than six months have passed since the date of birth, and she is not working.
    *   The borrower is entitled to defer mortgage payment dates according to this section for the period requested, not exceeding three months, no more than once in 12 consecutive months and no more than three times during the loan period.
2.  The payments whose repayment date was deferred will be spread over the remaining payments until the end of the original loan term. Therefore, the monthly payment after the deferral will increase (in addition to the fact that the monthly payment may change due to linkage differentials or interest rate changes, depending on the type of loans).
3.  For deferring the monthly repayment date according to this section, the borrower will be required to pay the bank a fee for changes to the loan agreement at the customer's request, in the amount specified in the tariff at the time of the loan request. As of the date of this guide, the fee is NIS 200.
    *   In the event of the borrower's death, the additional borrower / heirs are entitled to request deferral of mortgage payments for up to 12 months and at no cost.
4.  Deferral of the monthly repayment date for a housing loan according to this section will not affect the rights available to the borrower under the loan agreement or any law, including their rights to alternative housing or their right to appeal to the special committee according to the provisions of section 5A of the Housing Loans Law, 5752-1992.

## Early Repayment of the Mortgage

Do you have a sum of money that allows for partial or full repayment of the mortgage before its end date?
Early repayment is possible. However, for certain types of loans, you will pay early repayment fees, and the economic viability of the repayment should always be considered.

### Below is an overview of early repayment fees:

According to section 13 of the Banking Ordinance 1941, anyone who received a housing loan is entitled to repay it before the repayment date set when the loan was granted, and the bank is entitled to condition the early repayment of the housing loan on payment. The amount of payment the bank is entitled to collect is determined by the Governor of the Bank of Israel through the Banking Order (Early Repayment of Housing Loan), 5762-2002 (hereinafter: "the Order" or "the Banking Order"). The payment is intended, among other things, to mitigate the economic damage caused to the bank, if any, as a result of repaying the loan before the repayment date agreed upon between it and the customer.
Below is general information regarding the terms of early repayment of the loan, which will help you choose which loan track is suitable for you, when it is worthwhile to make an early repayment of the requested loan track, what fees are involved in the repayment, and how to save on them. Even after the loan disbursement (if granted), you can receive information to examine the viability of early repayment of your specific loan on the bank's website.

**Types of Early Repayment Fees and their Amounts:**

#### Operational Fee

Currently, the fee rate is NIS 60. In case of repayment through direct channels (internet/digital), a discount on the operational fee amount will be granted, resulting in a reduced fee payment. The exact amount of the fee will be determined according to the bank's tariff at the time of repayment and according to the repayment method.
An operational fee will be charged once for the early repayment of a loan, across its tracks, on a specific date.

#### Fee for Lack of Prior Notice:

0.1% of the amount being repaid.

**Please note** - this fee will not be charged if prior written notice is given (at the bank branch, by mail, fax, telephone hotline, or internet according to the repayment terms there). The notice must be given 10 days in advance of the early repayment but no more than 45 days before the date the customer set for executing the early repayment. The notice must specify the requested repayment amount and the expected repayment date.
**Pay attention,** a borrower who gave prior notice and did not make an early repayment will not be entitled to this benefit of non-collection of the fee for giving prior notice, upon early repayment made within 6 months from the date set for early repayment in the first notice, and the fee will be charged.
A fee for lack of prior notice will not apply if you are taking out a new loan at Bank Hapoalim to repay the existing loan at the bank (refinancing).
In the unfortunate event of the borrower's death, this fee will not be charged.

#### Capitalization Differential Fee (Economic Loss)

This fee is charged when, at the time of early repayment, the average interest rate as detailed below is lower than the interest rate applicable to the loan at the time of repayment or lower than the average interest rate as of the loan origination date, whichever is lower. The average interest rate is published by the Bank of Israel, and its change affects the fee amount. The fee calculation is performed according to capitalization rules and a formula determined by the Supervisor of Banks and published in the Banking Order.

##### For Fixed Interest Rate Loans

The fee will be the amount of the difference between:
*   The future payments the borrower wishes to repay early, discounted to the present value on the day of early repayment, according to the average interest rate as of that day;
*   And those same payments, discounted to the present value on the day of early repayment, according to the loan interest rate or the average interest rate at the time the loan was granted, whichever is lower.

An amount may be deducted from the resulting fee amount at the rate specified in the table below, based on the number of years that have passed since the loan origination date.

##### For Variable Interest Rate Loans

For the purpose of calculating the fee, the estimated principal balance on the date when a new interest rate applied or could have applied will be considered as the last future payment in the stream of future payments the borrower wishes to repay early. The fee will be the amount of the difference between:
*   The future payments the borrower wishes to repay early, discounted to the present value on the day of early repayment, according to the average interest rate as of that day;
*   And those same payments, discounted to the present value on the day of early repayment, according to the loan interest rate (i.e., the rate determined according to the mechanism for setting the interest rate specified in the loan agreement, at the last change date) or the average interest rate at the time the loan was granted, or the last interest rate change date, as applicable, whichever is lower.

An amount may be deducted from the resulting fee amount at the rate specified in the table below, based on the number of years that have passed since the loan origination date.

**Important to know!** In any case, an economic loss fee will not be charged for a variable interest rate loan where the interest rate change dates are not known in advance (e.g., in a loan track based on the Prime rate), or if they are known in advance but set for once a year or more frequently (every 10 months, 6 months, 3 months, etc.) or if the repayment is made on the interest rate change date. If the early repayment is made on the interest rate change date, only an operational fee will be charged". Therefore, it is recommended to plan the repayment date accordingly and considering the other relevant parameters for you.

**Discount Table for Capitalization Differential Fee:**

| Number of Years Passed Since Loan Origination | Up to 1 Year | 1-2 Years | 2-3 Years | 3-4 Years | 4-5 Years | Over 5 Years |
| :--------------------------------------------- | :----------- | :-------- | :-------- | :-------- | :-------- | :----------- |
| Bank-funded loan granted alongside a directed loan | -            | 10%       | 20%       | 30%       | 40%       | 40%          |
| Bank-funded loan (not alongside a directed loan) | -            | -         | -         | 20%       | 20%       | 30%          |

_The longer the loan is paid, the lower the economic loss fee becomes, as detailed in the table above. Therefore, it is recommended to check if you are close to a date of change in the applicable discount rate and plan the repayment date accordingly and considering the other relevant parameters for you. In case a gap is created in favor of the borrower, meaning the average interest rate at the time of early repayment is higher than the loan interest rate, or higher than the average interest rate at the time the loan was granted, whichever is higher, the other fees will be reduced accordingly, potentially down to their complete cancellation._

#### CPI-Linked Loan Fee ("Average Index Fee"):

This fee is charged for linkage differentials in loans linked to the Consumer Price Index. This payment constitutes partial compensation to the bank for the period during which the loan was active but was not indexed to the CPI due to the gap between the publication dates of the CPI".
The fee will be charged when the early repayment is made between the 1st and 15th of the month. Its calculation is the amount being repaid multiplied by half the average rate of change in the CPI over the last 12 indices published before the repayment day.
**Please note** you can save this fee if the loan repayment is made between the 16th of the month and the end of the month. However, on the 15th of the month, the balance of CPI-linked loans is updated according to the rise or fall of the index. Therefore, you should consider whether it is worthwhile to postpone the repayment until after the index publication or to prepay it before the index publication date.

#### Early Repayment Fee for Loan Track Including Deferred Payments until the End of the Loan Term - Bridging Loan:

The following applies to the following loan tracks where the payment deferral is for the loan principal only and the linkage differentials thereon, or for the principal, interest, and linkage differentials - Bridging Loan.

##### Upon early repayment of a fixed linked loan track including deferred payments as mentioned above:

If the loan term is up to (and including) 36 months, the borrower will be exempt from the economic loss fee. If the loan term is longer than 36 months, the borrower will be required to pay the economic loss fee.
Additionally, for any loan including deferred payments in this track, the borrower will be required to pay an operational fee and a fee for lack of prior notice (unless prior notice was given as detailed above). If the loan is repaid between the 1st and 15th of the month, the borrower will also be required to pay the average index fee".

##### Upon early repayment of a fixed non-linked loan track including deferred payments as mentioned above:

If the loan term is up to (and including) 36 months, the borrower will be exempt from the economic loss fee. If the loan term is longer than 36 months, the borrower will be required to pay the economic loss fee.
Additionally, for any loan including deferred payments in this track, the borrower will be required to pay an operational fee and a fee for lack of prior notice (unless prior notice was given as detailed above).

##### Upon early repayment of a variable prime track including deferred payments as mentioned above:

The borrower will not be required to pay the economic loss fee in case of repayment on the interest rate change date (in this loan, the interest rate change date is the monthly debit date) but will be required to pay an operational fee and also a fee for lack of prior notice (unless notice was given as detailed above).
